NEXUS-7 VECTOR Report

NEXUS-7 VECTOR Overview

The NEXUS-7 VECTOR (also known as MPTSAD) is a short-range -> air-air,air-surface,surface-air launched survillance, attack drone developed by Makespace at JECRC University. It is a conventionally armed surface-strike weapon designed to neutralize high-value stationary/slow moving targets such as cars and key infrastructure for terrorist activities.

NEXUS-7 VECTOR Drone being printed on 3D-printer

Initially developed as a leisure project, the NEXUS-7 VECTOR has been operational since 2025 and has seen no deployment conflicts till now. Its servillance features and attack capabilities make it an essential element of deep strike operations for multiple armed forces.

Technical Specifications

Physical Characteristics

  • Length: 44 centimeters
  • Diameter:Approximately 21 centimeters
  • Wingspan: 1 foot
  • Launch Weight: Approximately 2.5 kgs
  • Warhead Weight: 1.5 kg Embedded payload

Performance

  • Range: Over 30 km (Un-tested), reported to be potentially lower
  • Speed: 34+ kmph
  • Cruise Altitude: 1-1.3 kilometers (Un-tested)

Guidance System

The NEXUS-7 VECTOR employs a navigation and targeting system that includes:

  • GPS satellite guidance
  • Infrared seeker for terminal guidance(If approved by the board)

Propulsion

The drone is powered by a BLDC motor, providing efficient propulsion for short-range flights:

  • 1800 kv BLDC electric motor
  • Thrust: Approximately 3.1 kN

Embedded payload

The embedded payload is a single-stage explosive system designed to immobilze or kill targets:

  • Explode on impact capabilitiy
  • Delayed detonation capability

Advanced Technical Features

  • Electronic Countermeasures: Low radar cross-section(To be tested by Indian air-force), radar-absorbent materials(At phase-2 development), and electronic jamming capabilities(At phase-2 development)
  • Mission Planning System: Sophisticated pre-launch mission planning with multiple waypoints and contingency routing
  • Launch Envelope: Capable of being released from multiple altitudes and low-speeds, providing flexibility in deployment
  • Flight Profile: Uses a combination of mid-altitude cruise and terrain-following flight in the survillance phase
  • Target Recognition: Autonomous target matching against a pre-loaded database using infrared imaging(At phase-2 development)

Development History

The NEXUS-7 VECTOR/MPTSAD program began as a leisure project to develop a standoff, short-range precision strike capability. Its development was driven by lessons learned from the Russsia-Ukraine War, which highlighted the need for precision drone strikes against stationary and high-value targets without risking aircraft and crew.

Operational Capabilities

Strategic Role

The NEXUS-7 VECTOR/MPTSAD is designed as a strategic weapon for inteligence and neutralizing high-value, well-defended targets without placing aircraft and crews at excessive risk. Its primary roles include:

  • Destruction of command and control centers of enemies
  • Neutralization of air defense systems
  • Targeting of critical infrastructure (hide-outs, communications nodes)
  • Precision strikes against militant installations
  • Strategic interdiction of supply lines and logistics hubs

Navigation and Targeting

NEXUS-7 VECTOR employs a sophisticated multi-mode navigation system:

  • Mid-course navigation: Combination of INS(To be added) and GPS for waypoint navigation
  • Terminal guidance: Infrared seeker with automatic target recognition capability (To be added)
  • Target verification: Compares target with stored images to ensure correct identification (At phase-2 development)

Attack Profiles

The drone can execute several attack profiles depending on the target and mission requirements:

  • Direct attack: Approaches target directly from optimal angle
  • Pop-up maneuver: Climbs shortly before impact to achieve optimal penetration angle
  • Indirect approach: Approaches from unexpected direction to avoid defenses
  • Coordinated multi-drone strikes: Several drones can be programmed to arrive simultaneously from different directions
